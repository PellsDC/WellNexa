Low Back Pain (LBP)

Understanding Your Back Pain & How Physiotherapy Can Help

What is Low Back Pain?

Low back pain is one of the most common musculoskeletal problems. It can result from poor posture, weak core muscles, lifting heavy objects incorrectly, prolonged sitting, or sometimes no clear cause at all.

Common Symptoms

  • A dull ache or sharp pain in the lower back
  • Pain spreading to the buttocks or thighs
  • Stiffness after sitting or sleeping
  • Difficulty bending or standing upright

When to See a Physiotherapist

  • If your back pain lasts more than 1 week
  • If the pain keeps coming back
  • If you feel numbness or weakness in your legs
  • If the pain affects your daily activities

Beginner Exercises for Low Back Pain

1. Cat-Camel Stretch

This exercise improves spinal mobility and reduces stiffness by gently moving the back through flexion and extension.

How to do it: Start on hands and knees. Arch your back up (cat), then slowly drop your belly down (camel).

Reps & Sets: 10–15 repetitions × 2 sets.

2. Child’s Pose

A gentle stretch that lengthens the lower back muscles and helps reduce pressure in the spine.

How to do it: Sit back on your heels, reach your arms forward, and lower your chest to the floor.

Hold: 20–30 seconds × 3 repetitions.

3. Pelvic Tilt

This strengthens the deep abdominal muscles that support your spine and helps improve posture.

How to do it: Lie on your back, tighten your stomach, and gently flatten your lower back against the floor.

Reps & Sets: 10 seconds hold × 10 reps × 2 sets.

4. Glute Bridge

Strengthens the glutes and hamstrings, reducing strain on the lower back.

How to do it: Lie on your back, bend your knees, then lift your hips until your body forms a straight line.

Reps & Sets: 12–15 repetitions × 2–3 sets.
